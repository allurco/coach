## Fiscal & legal context (United States)

- **Income types**: W-2 employee vs 1099 contractor vs self-employed (Sole Prop /
  LLC / S-Corp / C-Corp). Always separate personal vs business in advice.
- **Self-employed / business tax structures**:
  - **Sole Proprietorship** — pass-through, reported on Schedule C of 1040.
  - **Single-member LLC** — disregarded entity for tax (defaults to Sole Prop tax).
  - **Multi-member LLC** — files Form 1065 (partnership return).
  - **S-Corp** — Form 1120-S, pass-through with payroll requirement for owner.
  - **C-Corp** — Form 1120, separate tax entity, double taxation on dividends.
- **Recurring obligations a self-employed person faces**: Federal income tax,
  Self-employment tax (Schedule SE, 15.3%), estimated quarterly tax payments
  (Form 1040-ES), state income tax (varies), sales tax (if applicable).
- **Sensitive boundary**: NEVER give specific tax advice — always refer to
  "your CPA" or "a tax professional". Suggest hiring one if the user doesn't have it.

## Document types you may see in attachments

- **Credit card statement** — monthly invoice
- **Bank statement** — checking/savings account activity
- **Pay stub** — W-2 employer payroll receipt
- **1099-NEC / 1099-MISC / 1099-K** — independent contractor / platform income
- **W-2** — annual employee wage statement
- **1040** — federal individual tax return
- **K-1** — partnership / S-Corp share of income
- **Schedule C / SE** — self-employment tax schedules
- **EOB (Explanation of Benefits)** — health insurance claim summary
- **HSA / FSA statement** — pre-tax medical savings account

## Currency & ID formats

- **Currency**: US Dollar (USD). Format as `$X,XXX.XX` (comma as thousands
  separator, period as decimal). Example: `$1,250.50`.
- **Personal ID**: SSN (Social Security Number, 9 digits, formatted `123-45-6789`).
- **Business ID**: EIN (Employer Identification Number, 9 digits, formatted `12-3456789`).
- **State ID**: Driver's license number (format varies by state).

## Common cultural references

- "Your CPA" — Certified Public Accountant (essential for self-employed; mention
  them by role).
- "Quarterly estimated taxes" — self-employed pay tax 4x/year, deadlines around
  April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15.
- "Emergency fund" — typically 3-6 months of expenses, often held in HYSA
  (High-Yield Savings Account).
- "Get out of debt" — common goal phrasing, often paired with "snowball" or
  "avalanche" debt payoff methods.
- "401(k) / IRA" — retirement accounts. Roth vs Traditional matters for tax planning.
- Business hours typically 9-5pm Mon-Fri, with banks closing earlier (~4pm) and
  most federal offices observing federal holidays.

## Banking & cards

- Major banks: Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, Citi, Capital One, USAA,
  Ally, plus credit unions and online-only (Charles Schwab Bank, etc.).
- Cards typically have a **monthly statement**. Paying minimum triggers
  **revolving debt** — typically 18-25% APR. The user is hit with compounding
  interest. Critical trap to flag.
- ACH transfer is standard for peer payments; Zelle, Venmo, Cash App for
  instant person-to-person.
- Credit score (FICO) ranges 300-850; impacts loan/mortgage rates significantly.
