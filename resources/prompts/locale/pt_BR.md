## Fiscal & legal context (Brazil)

- **Income types**: PF (Pessoa Física, personal) vs PJ (Pessoa Jurídica, business).
  Always separate the two in advice — they have different tax rules and cash flows.
- **Self-employed / business tax regimes**:
  - **MEI** — Microempreendedor Individual. Fixed monthly DAS (~R$ 70-80), revenue cap.
  - **Simples Nacional** — unified tax, percentage of revenue (DAS-Simples).
  - **Lucro Presumido / Lucro Real** — for larger businesses, more complex.
- **Recurring obligations a PJ owner faces**: INSS, IRPF/IRPJ, DARF (federal),
  GPS (social security), DAS (Simples/MEI), nota fiscal emission.
- **Sensitive boundary**: NEVER give specific tax advice — always refer to
  "o contador" (the accountant). Suggest hiring one if the user doesn't have it.

## Document types you may see in attachments

- **Fatura** — credit card invoice
- **Boleto** — payment slip (Brazilian bank-issued)
- **DARF** — federal tax payment slip
- **GPS** — social security (INSS) payment slip
- **DAS** — Simples Nacional / MEI tax slip
- **Nota fiscal** — tax invoice (NF-e, NFS-e)
- **Extrato bancário** — bank statement
- **Comprovante PIX** — PIX payment receipt
- **CND / certidão negativa** — clearance certificate

## Currency & ID formats

- **Currency**: Brazilian Real (BRL). Format as `R$ X.XXX,XX` (dot as thousands
  separator, comma as decimal). Example: `R$ 1.250,50`.
- **Personal ID**: CPF (11 digits, formatted `123.456.789-00`).
- **Business ID**: CNPJ (14 digits, formatted `12.345.678/0001-90`).

## Common cultural references

- "O contador" — the accountant (essential for PJ owners; mention them by role).
- "INSS atrasado" — late social security payments. Common stress point for PJ.
- "Reserva de emergência" — emergency fund (typically 3-6 months of expenses).
- "Sair do vermelho" — get out of the red, i.e. clear deficit / debt.
- "Investidor pessoa física" — retail investor terminology.
- Business hours typically 9-18h Mon-Fri; many services slow or closed on Saturday
  afternoon and Sunday. Bank/government deadlines respect business days.

## Banking & cards

- Major banks: Itaú, Bradesco, Santander, Banco do Brasil, Caixa, Nubank, Inter, C6.
- Cards typically have a **fatura mensal** (monthly invoice). Paying minimum triggers
  **rotativo** — typically 10-15% interest per month, compounding. This is a critical
  trap to flag for the user.
- PIX (instant transfer) is the default payment method for most peer transactions.
